---

## 📋 **Problem Statement**

House hunting in Nairobi is a nightmare for students and recent graduates:

- **Skyrocketing rents:** KSh 30k+ for basic 1-bedrooms
- **Scattered listings:** Informal posts on WhatsApp groups
- **Unreliable agents:** Wasting weeks of valuable time
- **No student focus:** Platforms ignore budget constraints (10k-50k range)

---

## 🎯 **Our Solution**

RentaNbo eases this with a local, student-focused app for affordable rentals in key neighborhoods:

- **Target areas:** Rongai, Pipeline, Kahawa, and more
- **Budget-friendly:** Bed-sitters and single rooms under KSh 50k
- **Student perks:** Shared WiFi, borehole water, roommate-friendly spaces
- **Direct contacts:** Connect with landlords/agents instantly

## 🎥 **How It Works - Step by Step**

### **Step 1: Download & Sign Up**
Download from Google Play Store. Enter your +254 Kenyan phone number and verify with the SMS OTP sent instantly. Add your name, preferred neighborhood (Rongai/Pipeline/Kahawa), and monthly budget (10k-50k KSh).

### **Step 2: Set Language Preference**
Toggle Swahili/English at the top-right corner for easy reading using Google ML Kit integration.

### **Step 3: Search Rentals**
Tap the search icon. Slide budget filter (e.g., 20k-25k). Check amenities (WiFi, borehole water). Select house type (bed-sitter, single room). The map auto-zooms to your area showing pins.

### **Step 4: Browse Listings**
Tap any pin to see photos, price, description, and landlord +254 contact. Swipe left/right through similar listings.

### **Step 5: Save & Get Alerts**
Heart icon to save favorites. Tap "Save This Search" for automatic SMS alerts on new matches like:
> *"New KSh 18k bed-sitter with WiFi in Kahawa! Tap to view."*

### **Step 6: Contact Landlord**
Tap "Message Landlord" for direct SMS. Schedule a virtual tour.

### **Step 7: Admin Posting**
Login as admin → "Post New Listing" → Add title, photos, price, neighborhood, amenities → Publish. Edit or delete anytime.
